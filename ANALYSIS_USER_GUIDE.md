## Tips for Best Results

### For Creating Presentations

- **Be specific** in your topic description
- **Choose the right style** for your audience:
  - Concise: Quick overviews (5-7 slides)
  - Detailed: In-depth content (10-15 slides)
  - Pitch: Business presentations (8 slides)
  - Educational: Learning materials (8 slides)

### For Getting Better Feedback

- **Create slides with content** (empty slides won't provide good analysis)
- **Use consistent formatting** across slides
- **Include a mix** of text, visuals, and different slide types
- **Review feedback critically** - take what helps, ignore what doesn't

### For Improving Presentations

- **Focus on one area at a time** rather than trying to fix everything
- **Balance visual and textual content** for maximum impact
- **Keep your target audience** in mind when making changes
- **Use regenerate** to see improvements over time

## FAQ

### Q: Will the analysis judge my content?

A: No! The analysis is constructive and supportive. It focuses on helping you improve, not criticizing. All feedback is specific and actionable.

### Q: Can I use the same feedback multiple times?

A: Yes! Regenerate analysis after making changes to see if you've addressed the concerns and get new suggestions for further improvement.

### Q: What if I disagree with the feedback?

A: That's okay! Use your judgment. The analysis is a tool to help, but you know your audience best. Implement feedback that makes sense for your goals.

### Q: Does the analysis change every time?

A: The AI looks at the same content, but may phrase feedback slightly differently. You'll see the core themes remain consistent.

### Q: Can I export the analysis?

A: Currently, you can screenshot or copy the feedback. Future versions may include PDF export.

### Q: What if there's an error?

A: If analysis fails, you'll see an error message. Go back to edit mode and try again. Make sure your presentation has content before analyzing.
